Frequently Asked Questions

UCO Management — Answered.

What is UCO management?

−
UCO (Used Cooking Oil) management is the collection, transport, and recycling of spent cooking oil from commercial kitchens. TruShine Environmental collects your used oil on a smart schedule, transports it to licensed processing facilities, and recycles it into renewable biodiesel — turning your kitchen waste into clean energy.

What is IoT tank monitoring?

−
Smart sensors installed on your UCO tanks transmit real-time fill level data. You see your tank status from any device. Our dispatch system automatically routes collection trucks when tanks reach optimal fill level. The sensors also detect unusual drops that indicate oil theft.

How does UCO recycling help my business?

+
UCO recycling eliminates disposal liability, prevents environmental violations, reduces theft risk, and supports sustainability goals. High-volume kitchens qualify for revenue sharing. For businesses with ESG reporting requirements, we provide documented environmental impact metrics including gallons recycled, carbon offset, and biodiesel production data.

How much does UCO management cost?

+
UCO management is contract-priced based on volume, number of locations, and collection frequency. Many high-volume kitchens qualify for revenue-sharing arrangements where we pay for your used oil based on market rates. Schedule a free assessment for a custom quote tailored to your operation.

Can I add UCO management to my existing TruShine contract?

+
Yes. Most UCO management clients already use TruShine for hood cleaning, grease trap, or other compliance services. Adding UCO to your existing contract gives you one vendor managing all kitchen compliance and environmental services — one invoice, one dashboard, one point of contact.
